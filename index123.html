About Me

Environmental Engineer | AI & IoT Systems Developer | University of Michigan Dual Enrollment

I'm a high school senior at Huron High School with a passion for environmental engineering and wastewater treatment innovation. My work focuses on using artificial intelligence and IoT systems to revolutionize how we approach water treatment, making existing systems more efficient, sustainable, and intelligent.

Through dual enrollment at the University of Michigan, I pursue graduate-level coursework in environmental data analysis while conducting hands-on research. My signature project involves developing an intelligent aeration system for microbial fuel cells in wastewater treatment, using machine learning optimization to maximize energy efficiency and treatment effectiveness.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of AI, IoT, and environmental systems—building smart sensors, predictive models, and automated control systems that can transform wastewater treatment plants into data-driven, self-optimizing facilities. From pollen forecasting to climate modeling, I apply computational approaches to solve real-world environmental challenges.

Beyond research, I'm an accomplished double bassist who has competed internationally and performed across the US and Europe. I also founded SteamFun4Kids, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to making STEM education accessible to underserved communities.

Engineering AI-IoT System for Energy Forecasting & Environmental Monitoring

Developed an AI-enabled aeration system for microbial fuel cells (MFCs) to optimize wastewater treatment and energy generation. Designed a self-powered microcontroller with integrated sensors and built a mobile application for real-time monitoring and AI-driven aeration control. The system uses machine learning algorithms to predict optimal aeration cycles, maximizing energy efficiency while improving treatment effectiveness. Won multiple awards including NASA Earth System Science Award, Navy ONR Medallion, and Stockholm Junior Water Prize Regional Award.

University of Michigan
Mentor: Dr. Kai Zhu

Environmental Data Science & Predictive Modeling

Developing neural network models for environmental forecasting (F1 = 0.684). Built interactive web applications for data visualization and deployed educational tools using real-time environmental sensor data. Awarded MIDAS PODS grant for continued IoT and machine learning research in environmental systems.

Michigan Math & Science Scholars (MMSS)

Ecological & Micrometeorological Instrumentation

Gained hands-on experience with professional ecological and meteorological field instruments, recording chlorophyll levels, air temperature, precipitation, albedo, wind speed, soil moisture, and relative humidity. Conducted research on surface albedo effects on material temperatures using laser thermometer, BTMETER BT-100, and Apogee SN500. Proposed urban design strategies to increase albedo. Visited W.K. Kellogg Biological Station to explore Long-Term Ecological Research (LTER) and Long-Term Agroecosystem Research (LTAR) sites. Earned certificates in Field Instruments and Meteorology.
